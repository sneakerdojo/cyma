# Octio — Strategic Business Case

This document is the long-form thinking behind Octio's product portfolio, go-to-market, and 12-month roadmap. It's written so a co-founder, advisor, investor, or new hire can read it cover-to-cover in 90 minutes and know exactly:

1. **What Octio is** (and isn't)
2. **What we sell** — every product mentioned on the marketing site, mapped to current operational status
3. **Who we sell to** and how big that market is
4. **How we win** vs. competitors playing in the same space
5. **The order we ship and sell in** — internal-first, then customer-facing, in the right sequence
6. **How we use our own products to sell them** — recursive go-to-market
7. **What 12 months looks like** from a financial and operational view
8. **Where we'd break** and how we'd respond

Each chapter is dated and versioned. Re-read the date on each page — strategy doesn't age well and we re-write when the world changes.

---

## How to read this

- **Tactical reader (engineer, designer):** skim chapters 4 + 6, then go deep on chapter 7
- **Strategic reader (founder, advisor):** read chapters 0 → 5 in order
- **Money reader (investor, banker):** chapters 0, 3, 9, 11
- **New hire:** chapters 0, 1, 4, 10 in week 1; the rest as you ramp

Every chapter ends with:
- **Key assumptions** — what we believe that would make us wrong if disproved
- **Open questions** — things we don't know yet but need to find out
- **Citations** — every external claim is sourced in the appendix

## A note on tone

This document is honest about what's built vs. marketed. Octio currently markets four autonomous AI products plus three services — only one (AI Lead Generation, running as Octo on octio.co.za) is genuinely operational. The 7-day plan referenced throughout closes that gap. The business case assumes the 7-day plan ships; if it doesn't, the timelines slip but the strategy still holds.

Bias: this is the founder's view (Simekani + Claude as research/drafting assistant). It's optimistic about execution and conservative about market. The risks chapter is where we put the doubts.
